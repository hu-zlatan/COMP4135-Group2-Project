\chapter{Requirements Engineering}

This chapter defines the final system requirements in terms of user needs, interaction scenarios, and the delivered functional and non-functional targets of the game. It also explains how the requirement set evolved from the earliest concept into the completed build, because the differences between the original design assumptions and the final implementation are important rather than accidental.

\section{Requirements Elicitation}

Three stakeholder perspectives shaped the requirement set.

\wcell{2.6cm}{Designer} & \wcell{4.5cm}{Positioning, range, timing, and action order should remain visible and tunable.} & \wcell{4.6cm}{The final build uses a small card set and deterministic enemy behaviour to keep balancing readable.} \\

These perspectives established the earliest design baseline: one compact tactical session for teenage players, readable enough for a first-time user, and small enough to be implemented and validated properly. That baseline remained stable, but several requirements changed in form as the team moved from concept diagrams to a playable system.

\section{Requirement Evolution}

The finished game differs in several important ways from the earliest design assumption, but the changes are coherent rather than contradictory. The original concept emphasised tactical combat on a small board. The delivered build still does that, but the implementation path showed that a battle-only prototype was not enough for a complete submission or for a readable first-time experience. As a result, several requirements evolved from provisional ideas into explicit user-facing obligations.

\begin{table}[H]
\centering
\small
\begin{tabular}{lllll}
\toprule
\wcell{2.2cm}{\textbf{Area}} & \wcell{2.5cm}{\textbf{Initial design assumption}} & \wcell{2.6cm}{\textbf{Delivered form}} & \wcell{2.8cm}{\textbf{Reason for change}} & \wcell{2.1cm}{\textbf{Validation evidence}} \\
\midrule
\wcell{2.2cm}{Session flow} & \wcell{2.5cm}{The earliest playable target focused on entering battle directly.} & \wcell{2.6cm}{The final build includes title, battle, and result states.} & \wcell{2.8cm}{The battle slice needed clearer entry, completion, replay, and menu return behaviour.} & \wcell{2.1cm}{Flow tests and PlayMode smoke} \\
\wcell{2.2cm}{HUD feedback} & \wcell{2.5cm}{Status text and log output were considered sufficient for the prototype.} & \wcell{2.6cm}{The final HUD exposes prompts, energy, disabled-card reasons, and world-space health bars.} & \wcell{2.8cm}{Playable smoke showed that hidden state and log-only feedback slowed understanding.} & \wcell{2.1cm}{`BattleUITests`, `CardButtonViewTests`, smoke} \\
\wcell{2.2cm}{Card variety} & \wcell{2.5cm}{The first card set only needed the minimum actions to prove the turn loop.} & \wcell{2.6cm}{The final build adds `Dash`, `Long Strike`, and `Recover` to the base actions.} & \wcell{2.8cm}{The battle needed slightly deeper tactical choice without broadening into deckbuilding.} & \wcell{2.1cm}{Card and deck tests, manual battle smoke} \\
\wcell{2.2cm}{Presentation} & \wcell{2.5cm}{Primitive units and minimal UI were acceptable while the core loop was still unstable.} & \wcell{2.6cm}{The final build uses themed UI, dressed boards, and silhouette-based unit presentation.} & \wcell{2.8cm}{A submission-ready slice needed stronger readability and visual cohesion.} & \wcell{2.1cm}{Visual smoke and runtime checks} \\
\wcell{2.2cm}{Validation} & \wcell{2.5cm}{Early validation focused mainly on proving core battle correctness.} & \wcell{2.6cm}{The final package combines EditMode tests, PlayMode smoke, and issue-driven UX smoke.} & \wcell{2.8cm}{Later UI and flow work introduced player-visible defects that required more than logic-only tests.} & \wcell{2.1cm}{Execution summary, smoke records, issue closure} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\caption{How the delivered system differs from the earliest design assumptions.}
\label{tab:requirements-evolution}
\end{table}

The final specifications should be regarded as a refined version of the original intent. The core objective of the project has always been compact tactical card battles targeted at teenage users. As issues emerged during the development process and new ideas came to light, the requirements were iterated accordingly.

\wcell{1.5cm}{FR-08} & \wcell{3.6cm}{The runtime HUD shall expose key battle state clearly.} & \wcell{3.8cm}{The final HUD shows prompts, hand, energy, battle log, card availability, and world-space health bars.} & \wcell{2.2cm}{`BattleUITests`, `CardButtonViewTests`, HUD smoke.} \\

\section{Requirements Validation}
